Remember when 25 cents seemed like a lot of money? A couple of quarters could buy an ice-cream cone!

Money is a great way to get students interested in math. Here's a good discussion to have with your students...

How many different words do you know for money?

  • Formal answers include "cash", "change", "coin", and "bill". Advanced responses include "salary", "currency", "greenback", and "payment".

  • Informal answers include "loot", "buck", "Benjamins", "clams", "moolah", and "dough".

  • Don't forget to honor international examples like:
    • "Dinero" (Spanish)
    • "Salapi" (Tagalog)
    • "Euro" (European)
    • "Yen" (Japanese)
    • "Renminbi (Chinese)...not to be confused with "Yuan" which is a unit of currency, like the dollar
